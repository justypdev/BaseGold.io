BaseGold vs Bitcoin: How a 10,000-Token Coin Compares

By the BaseGold Foundation · June 6, 2026 · 6 min read

Bitcoin defined digital scarcity with a 21 million cap. BaseGold takes the idea in the opposite direction: a fixed supply of just 10,000 tokens that actively shrinks. Here's how the two compare — and where they fundamentally differ.

Supply: 10,000 vs 21,000,000

Bitcoin will ever only have 21,000,000 coins. BaseGold caps out at 10,000 tokens. That's roughly 2,100x fewer units (21,000,000 ÷ 10,000 = 2,100). Scarcity is the headline difference, and it's not close.

Burning vs. Mining

This is the philosophical split. Bitcoin mines new coins into circulation, growing the supply over time until it reaches its cap — a disinflationary schedule. BaseGold does the inverse: it burns tokens out of existence from day one through six burn mechanisms. One supply curve trends up to a ceiling; the other trends down from one.

Speed and Fees

BaseGold lives on the Base network, an Ethereum Layer 2. That means fast confirmations (on the order of seconds) and very low transaction fees, compared with Bitcoin's roughly 10-minute block times and variable fees. Different networks, built for different purposes.

Bitcoin is the original digital scarcity. BaseGold is an experiment in extreme scarcity — far fewer units, and a supply engineered to fall rather than grow.

So, Is BaseGold "Digital Gold"?

BaseGold borrows gold's core property — scarcity — and pushes it further with a tiny, shrinking supply. But it is its own asset with its own risks, not a Bitcoin replacement. Bitcoin's security, liquidity, and track record are unmatched. BaseGold is a younger, experimental project betting that mechanical, ever-increasing scarcity is a compelling design. Both can be interesting for entirely different reasons.
